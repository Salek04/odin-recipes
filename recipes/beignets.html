Beignets Recipe

Description

A beignet is traditionally a deep-fried pastry. There are a few different types of beignets, but the most popular variety in the U.S. is topped with an ample amount of powdered sugar and served for breakfast. New Orleans is famous for its beignets

Ingredients

  • Yeast
  • Evaporated milk
  • sugars
  • Eggs
  • Salt
  • Flour
  • Vegetable shortening
  • Oil

Steps

  1. Activate the yeast, then mix in the evaporated milk, white sugar, eggs, salt, and four cups of flour to form a dough.
  2. Add the shortening and the remaining flour. Chill the dough.
  3. Roll the dough out and cut it into squares.
  4. Fry the dough in batches, then top with confectioners' sugar.
